## The problem

Smart-home appliances promise to make the kitchen more convenient, but a
connected refrigerator usually means buying a new one. Meanwhile, households
keep throwing away food simply because it is hard to remember what is in the
fridge and when it expires. We wanted the smart-refrigerator experience at a
price any family could afford — and without replacing the appliance they
already own.

## What I built

FridgePi is a low-cost smart refrigerator system built around a Raspberry Pi
that installs inside an existing fridge. A light sensor detects when the door
opens and triggers a camera to capture what is inside; a second camera with an
IR sensor lets you "scan" a new item just by holding it up to the device. Every
capture syncs through Google Firebase to a companion Android app, where each
item carries a photo, an expiration date, a quantity, and a storage location.
Before something expires, the app pushes a reminder to cook or eat it. A
touchscreen GUI on the Pi manages the same food list on the device itself.

As team lead, I managed the development schedule, designed the hardware
architecture, and built the Qt application that runs on the Raspberry Pi. I
researched and selected the sensors and components, designed the API between
the GUI and the database, and delivered the final project presentation.

## Tech

Qt/C++ for the on-device GUI, Python on the Raspberry Pi, an Android companion
app, a Node.js back-end, and Google Firebase for image storage and real-time
sync across devices.
