## The problem

A portfolio asks visitors to hunt through sections to learn who I am and what I
have built. I wanted a more direct way to answer the common questions —
"what have you worked on?", "how do I reach you?" — without making people read
the whole page first.

## What I built

I embedded a conversational assistant directly on the site using
[Botpress](https://botpress.com/), an open-source chatbot framework. The bot ran
on a self-hosted Node.js instance and loaded into the page through Botpress's
web-chat widget, giving visitors a small chat window to ask questions and get
guided to the right part of the site.

## Tech

Botpress running on a self-hosted Node.js server, embedded in the site with its
JavaScript web-chat widget.

## Outcome

The chatbot was an experiment on an earlier version of this portfolio — a way
to explore conversational interfaces on my own site. It has since been retired
(the embed is preserved, commented out, in the legacy site's source), but it
was a fun testbed for the messaging-bot work that also shows up in
[WatchdogPi](/projects/watchdogpi).
