## The problem

Getting locked out is more than an inconvenience — people have been hurt trying
to climb into their own apartments after forgetting a key. I wanted a doorbell
that could show me who was at the door from anywhere and let me open it without
carrying keys or installing yet another app.

## What I built

WatchdogPi is a smart doorbell built on a Raspberry Pi Zero, small enough to
replace an existing doorbell. A Pi camera module captures whoever is at the
door (and can be swapped for an IR module for low light), while an attached IR
transmitter drives my building's access-control system to open the door
downstairs. Instead of a custom app, the controls live in chat: LINE and
Telegram bots relay commands to a back-end server, so anyone can operate it
from a messaging app they already use every day.

![WatchdogPi system architecture: the Raspberry Pi, camera, and IR transmitter talk to an AWS EC2 back-end and a NAS server, controlled through LINE and Telegram bots](/projects/watchdogpi-cover.jpg)

Photos and commands flow between the Raspberry Pi and an AWS EC2 back-end, with
captured videos and photos archived to a NAS server. When the server receives
an "open" command from a bot, the Pi fires the IR signal to release the door.

## Tech

A Raspberry Pi Zero with a camera module and IR transmitter, Python on the
device, a JavaScript/PHP back-end on AWS EC2, a NAS server for media, and LINE
and Telegram bots as the control surface.

## Outcome

WatchdogPi works against my own apartment's access-control system, opening the
downstairs door from a chat message. Because the design is tuned to one
building, it is not plug-and-play everywhere: IR range weakens on higher floors
and, in rare cases, the open signal has to be sent twice. It remains an evolving
hobby project rather than a finished product.
